What is a Cooperative Society?

The word “cooperative” literally means working together. A cooperative society is a voluntary association where members organize under democratic management to improve their household and business conditions. Members pool capital and operate primarily to serve one another, putting collective economic progress above individual self-interest.

Formation and governance

  • Act: Formed under the Madhya Pradesh Cooperative Societies Act, 1960.
  • Registration: Registered at the Office of the Registrar of Cooperative Societies.
  • Management: Managed by an Executive Committee of 11 members, elected on democratic equality.
  • Policy making: Policies are decided in members’ meetings; implementation is the committee’s responsibility.
  • Bye-laws: Detailed rules specifying how the society operates.

Key features

  • Voluntary: Members can join or leave at will.
  • Open membership: Open to all who share its aims, without discrimination.
  • Finance: Capital contributed by members.
  • Purpose: Equal treatment, with “Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas” at the core.

Journey and milestones

  • Inception: 08-09-1995
  • Service span: 29+ years
  • Members: 2000+ active
  • Turnover: ₹3.5 crore
  • Loans disbursed: ~₹20 crore to 5000 members
  • Fixed deposits: FD facility available to members
