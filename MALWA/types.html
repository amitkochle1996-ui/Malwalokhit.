Types of cooperative societies

Consumer cooperatives

Provide goods to consumers at fair prices by purchasing directly from producers and eliminating middlemen. Examples: Kendriya Bhandar, Apna Bazaar, Super Bazaar.

Producer cooperatives

Support small producers with raw materials, machinery, tools, and equipment to safeguard their interests.

Agricultural cooperatives

Provide improved seeds, fertilizers, farm tools, credit, irrigation, and storage to member farmers.

Marketing cooperatives

Formed by small producers/manufacturers who can’t sell on their own; the society collects members’ goods and sells them. Example: Indore Cooperative Milk Union (Amul products distribution).

Financial cooperatives

Offer financial assistance and loans at reasonable interest, pooling funds from members. Examples: Gram Seva Cooperative Society, Urban Cooperative Bank.

Housing cooperatives

Acquire land, build houses/flats, and allot them to members.
